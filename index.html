Search Engines

  • Google: The most popular search engine
  • Bing: Microsoft's alternative to Google
  • Yahoo: A veteran web portal and directory
  • DuckDuckGo: A privacy-focused option
  • Ecosia: A green choice that plants trees
  • Wolfram Alpha: A computational knowledge engine
  • Ask: A question-and-answer based service
  • Yandex: A dominant force in Russia and beyond
  • Baidu: China's leading web platform
  • Naver: South Korea's top online resource
  • Qwant: A European challenger that respects privacy
  • Startpage: A private way to use Google's results
  • Dogpile: A meta-search engine that combines multiple sources
  • Swisscows: A family-friendly and semantic service
  • Gibiru: An uncensored and anonymous platform
  • OneSearch: A Verizon-owned service that promises no tracking
  • Search Encrypt: A service that encrypts your queries
  • Sogou: A Chinese service that offers web, image, and voice search
  • Searx: An open-source and customizable meta-search engine
  • Mojeek: A UK-based service that has its own crawler and index
  • Gigablast: An independent and scalable service that supports Boolean operators
  • MetaGer: A German service that combines results from over 20 sources
  • Oscobo: A UK-based service that does not store or share your data
  • Hotbot: A service that lets you choose from four different privacy levels
  • Lycos: A pioneer service that also offers email, hosting, and social networking
  • WebCrawler: A service that aggregates results from Google and Yahoo
  • Info.com: A service that provides results from multiple sources and verticals
  • Excite: A service that offers web search, email, news, and other features
  • LookSmart: A service that provides web search, shopping, and directory services
  • SlideShare: A service that allows you to search and share presentations
  • Creative Commons: A service that helps you find and use free and legal content
  • Internet Archive: A service that preserves and provides access to historical web pages
  • Wikipedia: A service that offers a free and collaborative online encyclopedia
  • Wikibooks: A service that offers a collection of free and open-content textbooks
  • Wiktionary: A service that offers a multilingual and free online dictionary
  • Wikiquote: A service that offers a collection of free and online quotations
  • Wikisource: A service that offers a library of free and online source texts
  • Wikinews: A service that offers a free and online news source
  • Wikiversity: A service that offers a free and online learning platform
  • Wikivoyage: A service that offers a free and online travel guide
  • Wikidata: A service that offers a free and online knowledge base
  • Wikimedia: A service that offers a free and online media repository
  • IMDb: A service that offers information and reviews on movies, TV shows, and celebrities
  • Rotten Tomatoes: A service that offers ratings and reviews on movies and TV shows
  • Metacritic: A service that offers scores and reviews on movies, TV shows, games, and music
  • Goodreads: A service that offers recommendations and reviews on books
  • AllMusic: A service that offers information and reviews on music and artists
  • Discogs: A service that offers a database and marketplace for music
  • Last.fm: A service that offers personalized music recommendations and scrobbling
  • Pandora: A service that offers internet radio and music streaming
  • Spotify: A service that offers music streaming and podcasts
  • YouTube: A service that offers video sharing and streaming
  • Vimeo: A service that offers video hosting and streaming
  • Dailymotion: A service that offers video sharing and streaming
